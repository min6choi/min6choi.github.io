The main purpose of this project is to help people who are new to a certain philosophy book have a basic understanding of the content of the book. Many philosophical books cannot predict the content of a book from its title or table of contents. And it is expected that these difficulties will be seen in many other academic books as well. So, we organized the project to classify the entire text of a specific book(we use â€˜The Ethics of Informationâ€™ by Luciano Floridi this time) of with Louvain's algorithm and see the associations between words at a glance.

 As a student who has studied this book in class, I personally could not easily understand the new definitions of the words by the author of the book or the connections between words and words. So, first, we visually display important words in the book according to the size of the circle which we call node, and make the first sentence where each word is located appear when the node is clicked so that the use of words in the book can be accessed more directly.
